IFRS 9, which stands for International Financial Reporting Standards 9, is a framework developed by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) for calculating impairments in financial instruments. It aims to provide a more forward-looking approach to assessing credit losses on financial assets, compared to the previous standard, IAS 39. The impairment calculation is based on a three-stage model: Stage 1, Stage 2, and Stage 3.Each stage reflects a different level of credit quality deterioration.

In Stage 1, when a financial instrument is initially recognized, a 12-month expected credit loss (ECL) is calculated. It represents the probability-weighted estimate of credit losses that will occur within the next 12 months.

If there is a significant increase in credit risk since initial recognition, the financial instrument is moved to Stage 2. In Stage 2, a lifetime ECL is calculated, which represents the expected credit losses over the entire contractual life of the instrument. If there is objective evidence of default, the instrument is classified as Stage 3, and the lifetime ECL is also calculated.

The calculation considers historical credit loss experience, economic factors, and the borrower's creditworthiness. The impairment assessment is regularly updated, and transparency is ensured through disclosure of methodologies and assumptions.
